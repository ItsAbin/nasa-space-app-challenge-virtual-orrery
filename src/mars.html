MARS

Mars, often referred to as the "Red Planet," is the fourth planet from the Sun in our
solar system. It has a thin atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide, with
traces of nitrogen and argon. Mars is about half the size of Earth, with a diameter
of approximately 6,779 kilometers (4,212 miles). Its surface is rocky, with mountains,
valleys, and large dust storms that can last for months.

Here are some notable features of Mars:
Color: The reddish appearance comes from iron oxide, or rust, on its surface.
Moons: Mars has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos, thought to be captured
asteroids.
Surface: It has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, and a vast
canyon system, Valles Marineris.
Water: While liquid water can't exist for long on the surface today, there is evidence
that Mars once had flowing rivers and lakes, and water ice has been found beneath
the surface.
Temperature: Mars is much colder than Earth, with an average surface temperature
of about -80°F (-60°C), though it can range from a warm 70°F (20°C) during the day
near the equator to -195°F (-125°C) at night.
