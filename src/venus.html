VENUS

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often called Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size and composition. However, its atmosphere is vastly different, with thick clouds of sulfuric acid and a dense carbon dioxide layer that causes extreme greenhouse effects. Venus has surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead, reaching around 465°C (869°F). It rotates very slowly and in the opposite direction to most planets, with one day on Venus lasting longer than its year. The planet has no moons and is known for being the brightest natural object in the night sky after the Moon.
Size (Diameter): 12,104 km (a little smaller than Earth)
Mass: 81.5% of Earth’s mass
Distance from the Sun: 108 million km (or 67 million miles)
Atmosphere: Mostly carbon dioxide (96.5%) and some nitrogen (3.5%), with clouds of sulfuric acid.
