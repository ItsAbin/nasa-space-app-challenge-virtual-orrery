Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and an ice giant, known for its striking blue-green color due to methane in its atmosphere. It has a unique axial tilt of about 98 degrees, causing extreme seasonal changes. With a diameter of approximately 31,518 miles (50,724 kilometers) and a mass about 14.5 times that of Earth, it has 27 known moons, including Titania and Oberon. Uranus also features a faint ring system and was discovered in 1781 by William Herschel.

Here are some notable features of Uranus:
Size: Diameter of about 31,518 miles (50,724 kilometers).
Mass: Approximately 14.5 times that of Earth, or about 8.68 × 10²⁵ kilograms.
Distance from the Sun: Average of about 1.9 billion miles (3.0 billion kilometers).
Atmospheric Composition: Primarily composed of hydrogen (about 83%), helium (about 15%), and methane (about 2%), along with trace amounts of other gases like ammonia and water.
