The central star of our solar system is the Sun, which supports warmness and light to make life on Earth. It is basically composed of hydrogen about 74% and helium about 24%. Its surface temperature is approximately 5,500°C or 9,932°F and that of the core reaches approximately 15 million degrees centigrade or 27 million degrees Fahrenheit. The Sun functions as an energy-generating star through nuclear fusion; it changes hydrogen into helium. The Sun is 1.39 million km or 864,000 miles in diameter and about 109 times in diameter compared to Earth.
