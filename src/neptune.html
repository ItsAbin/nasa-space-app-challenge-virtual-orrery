NEPTUNE

Neptune is the eighth, and one of the gas giants, with a deep blue color due to methane in its atmosphere. This gas giant boasts wind speed to be the fastest in the solar system, with a speed of over 1,200 miles per hour. Neptune also boasts large storms and has 14 known moons, of which Triton is the largest and unique for its retrograde orbit. It was discovered in 1846 and named after the Roman god of the sea.

Here are some notable features of Neptune:
Size: Diameter of about 30,598 miles (49,244 kilometers).
Mass: Approximately 17 times that of Earth, or about 1.02 × 10²⁶ kilograms.
Distance from the Sun: Average of about 2.7 billion miles (4.3 billion kilometers).
Atmospheric Composition: Primarily composed of hydrogen (about 80%), helium (about 19%), and methane (about 1%), with traces of other gases like ammonia and water.
