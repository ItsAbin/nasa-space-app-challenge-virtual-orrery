SATURN

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is especially known for its striking rings, consisting of ice and rock particles. It is a gas giant, consisting mostly of hydrogen and helium, and is the second biggest planet in the solar system, after Jupiter. Saturn has a dense atmosphere with powerful winds and storms. At least 83 satellites are known, which varies from the largest one known to date, Titan, described as having a thick atmosphere with liquid lakes.

Size (Diameter): 116,460 km (about 9.5 times Earth's diameter)
Mass: 5.68 × 10²⁶ kg (95 times Earth's mass)
Distance from the Sun: 1.43 billion km (886 million miles)
Atmosphere: Mostly hydrogen (96%) and helium (3%), with traces of methane, ammonia, and other gases.
